About Cotton

Cotton is a globally important fibre crop grown for its lint and seed. Yields depend on variety, soil fertility, planting dates, and management practices including irrigation and pest control. Understanding these drivers helps in making better agronomic decisions.

Climate & Yield

Temperature, rainfall distribution, and heat units during critical growth stages strongly influence cotton development. The model captures such signals where available in the dataset to improve forecast quality.

How Prediction Works

We preprocess inputs (impute missing values, scale numeric features, one-hot encode categoricals) and use a LightGBM regressor to model complex interactions. This approach balances speed and predictive accuracy for practical use on farms and advisory services.

Practical uses

Estimated yields can inform irrigation scheduling, fertilizer application planning, and market supply forecasts. Use local measurements to get the most accurate predictions.
